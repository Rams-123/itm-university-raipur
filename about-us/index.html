REGISTRAR’s MESSAGE

Dear Future Leaders,

ITM University, Raipur, (ITMUR) has always aspired for a holistic and inclusive growth of the individuals as well as the society and hence, today it has earned the reputation of an emerging higher education institution in the region. Established in 2012 by Chhattisgarh Private Universities (Establishment and Operations Amendment) Act 2011[4] Section 9 (2) by ITM Society Raipur, the varsity has regularly been investing in human resources, creating processes and managing funds judiciously. It has stood firm to the growing demands by pursuing a synergetic and coherent structure.

The ITMUR adopted the Choice Based Credit System (CBCS) in conformity with the UGC and AICTE guideline from 2018 providing academic flexibilities to select courses from a basket of Program and Open Electives offered by Engineering, Architecture, Life Sciences, Management, Commerce, Hotel Management and Law programs. The University regularly organizes review, revision, and upgradation of the teaching courses and academic curriculum through wide consultation with all the stakeholders, including the Board of Studies (BoS) of each Department.

ITMUR also believes that it has a role in the transformation of society. It is with this perspective that we have adopted 3 villages, including 2 tribal villages, in Chhattisgarh, and our students today engage themselves in several civic and societal issues to transform the lives of these villagers through social responsibility forums and National Service Scheme.

At ITMUR, we firmly believe that we have an obligation to the profession, society, and nation that can be best realized by imparting quality education, enabling the pupils with confidence and conviction, and above all, creating characters to perform and contribute, pursue excellence in the profession and conduct life with a heart filled with compassion and dedication.

The ITMUR would remain adaptive to new ideas and strategies to meet and remain relevant to the growing aspirations of our young India by aiming at initiatives like start-ups and incubation centres, human development centres, offering facilities for women empowerment, social development and environmental protection to be enhanced for exploring new horizons in higher education and research. Thus, I take this opportunity to invite you to be a part of this ‘young, exuberant and illustrious’ ITMUR's journey for building an academically elite, professionally sound, and socially empowering institutional structure which remains flexible and fundamentally strong to face the upcoming environmental challenge.

Mr. Sourav Chatterjee

Registrar, ITM University Raipur

Quality Policy

Preamble:

The National Assessment and Accreditation Council has recommended that all universities create an Internal Quality Assurance Cell (IQAC). ITM University, located in Raipur, was founded in February 2012. The Internal Quality Assurance Cell (IQAC) of the University was established on June 19, 2017, to ensure and maintain high-quality standards. The IQAC is responsible for strategizing, directing, and overseeing the implementation of Quality Assurance (QA) and Quality Enhancement (QE) initiatives at the University. The primary responsibility of the IQAC is to guide the University's endeavors and initiatives in sustaining academic excellence and fostering strong internal management.

Quality Policy:

The objective is to establish and implement a robust quality system that encompasses teaching, research, consultation, continuing education, and core and support services. This system will provide accountability to stakeholders through self-assessment and ongoing enhancement.

Quality Objectives:

  • To establish & implement a robust quality system encompassing teaching, research, consultancy, continued education, and focus on the core and support functions to ensure accountability to stakeholders through self-evaluation and continuous improvement.
  • The objective is to enhance the academic and administrative performance of an institution by fostering a culture of excellence and implementing effective methods. This entails the process of carefully choosing and instructing internal quality monitoring personnel to supervise the implementation of quality system protocols. Consistent evaluations and subsequent remedial measures are implemented to achieve ongoing improvement.
  • To develop an approach to promote pedagogical innovation and creative teaching techniques among staff through staff development. The University aims to utilize electronic resources for instructing and overseeing the Learning Management System (LMS).
  • The institution aims to participate in multiple accreditation processes, including NIRF, NAAC, NBA, and international accreditation.
  • To supervise the progress of students' academic development and offer guidance and assistance.
  • To provide students with informational seminars, workshops, and educational visits through the Career Guidance and Placement Cell.
  • To urge Faculty members to submit articles in foreign journals and create mechanisms for receiving input on faculty, curriculum, institutional performance, library, and hostel amenities.
  • To develop a robust feedback system to contribute inputs from parents, graduates, and employers.
  • To develop a system that fosters an entrepreneurial mindset and promotes student-led initiatives.
  • To engage students regularly in community service and promote awareness of national matters. To encourage the institution to forge relationships with industry and research organizations, strengthen alliances, and produce revenue through consultation services.
  • To ensure that faculty development initiatives and partnerships with international universities enable a wide range of exchange programs.

Quality Assurance Strategies / Activities:

  • To build and effectively implement a strong quality system that includes teaching, research, consultancy, continued education, and also focuses on the core and support functions to provide accountability to stakeholders through self-evaluation and continuous improvement.
  • To establish a methodical and purposeful approach to enhance the academic and administrative performance of the school.
  • To facilitate the improvement of institutional functioning by promoting the internalization of a quality culture and the establishment of best practices.
  • To select and educate a team of internal quality monitoring members to oversee the successful execution of quality system operations.
  • To guarantee that the specified Processes and Procedures are regularly evaluated and implemented appropriately to achieve continual enhancement.
  • To ensure the maintenance of appropriate paperwork to substantiate the Quality Processes and Procedures implemented.

Functions of IQAC:

  • To build and effectively implement a strong quality system that includes teaching, research, consultancy, continuous education, and a specific emphasis on the key aspects.
  • The objective is to establish and efficiently execute a robust quality system that encompasses teaching, research, consultancy, and ongoing education, and prioritizes both essential and auxiliary roles to ensure accountability to stakeholders through self-assessment and ongoing enhancement.
  • To implement a systematic and deliberate strategy to improve the academic and administrative performance of the institution.
  • To enhance the operation of institutions by encouraging the adoption of a culture of excellence and the use of optimal methods.
  • To recruit and train a group of internal quality monitoring personnel to supervise the effective implementation of quality system activities.
  • To ensure that the designated Processes and Procedures are consistently assessed and that suitable corrective measures are adopted to achieve ongoing improvement.
  • To ensure the proper documentation is maintained to support the implementation of Quality Processes and Procedures.

Benefits of IQAC

  1. The IQAC will facilitate and contribute to the following:
    • Ensuring a higher level of clarity and focus on the functioning of the institution to enhance quality.
    • Promoting the internalization of a culture of quality.
    • Enhancing and coordinating various institutional activities and institutionalizing all best practices.
  2. Provide a solid foundation for informed decision-making to enhance institutional operations.
  3. Serve as a dynamic framework for implementing quality improvements in higher education institutions.
  4. Establish a systematic approach for documenting and communicating throughout the organization.

IIQAC Composition

  1. Chairperson: Head of the Institution
  2. Teachers to represent all levels (Assistant Professor and Associate Professor) (Three to eight)
  3. One member from the Management
  4. The senior administrative officer (Office Superintendent/Manager)
  5. One nominee each from the Local Society/Trust, Students and Alumni
  6. One nominee each from the Employer/Industrialists/Stakeholders
  7. One of the senior teachers as the Coordinator of the IQAC

Term of Members & Meetings of IQAC

  1. The tenure of the Office of the Members of the IQAC shall be three years and may be extended by the Chairperson.
  2. All the meetings of IQAC shall always be chaired by the Vice Chancellor and in his / her absence, by any one of the nominees of the Vice Chancellor, Pro-Chancellor.
  3. IQAC shall meet at least Four Times a year on dates, times, and places decided by the Chairperson / Vice Chancellor/ Pro-Chancellor.
  4. The Agenda for IQAC meetings shall be prepared by the Deputy Director, IQAC, under the instructions of and with the prior approval of the Pro-Chancellor, Vice-Chancellor, or Chairperson.
  5. An accurate record of proceedings (minutes) from each meeting shall be prepared and maintained by the Deputy Director, IQAC, at the office of the Vice-Chancellor.
  6. To facilitate collaboration, decentralization, and the smooth decision-making and implementation of quality enhancement, the IQAC shall function at two levels, namely
    • a. IQAC Steering Committee: This committee shall ensure consistent quality improvement in the University by meeting twice a year. It will not only provide suggestions for quality improvement of the university but also review the activities of the IQAC in the University. (Composition of this committee is as per the guidelines of NAAC).
    • b. IQAC Team: The IQAC would originate the planning for Quality improvement and its proper implementation, in consultation with the Executive Committee. The documentation of all activities and submission of reports to external bodies come under this committee. (Composition of this committee is as per the guidelines of NAAC).
